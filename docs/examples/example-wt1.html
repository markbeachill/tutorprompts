WT1 — Clarity Clinic v4.0

Please paste one sentence, a few sentences, or one paragraph you want to make clearer.

User

It is widely acknowledged in the literature that there are many factors which can be considered relevant when looking at this area. This means brands struggle to know which of these they should respond to.

Chatbot

Your text:

It is widely acknowledged in the literature that there are many factors which can be considered relevant when looking at this area. This means brands struggle to know which of these they should respond to.

The main clarity problem is that the reader cannot yet see the real subject or action. Phrases like “many factors”, “this area”, “these” and “respond to” are too general, so the sentence sounds academic but does not yet tell the reader what the problem actually is.

A clearer version will need to name the area and the factors more directly. You do not need to make it more complex; you need to make the thinking more visible.

Made-up example:

Before:

It is often argued that there are many issues involved in this situation. This means schools do not know which ones to address.

After:

Schools may struggle to prioritise attendance problems because pupils miss lessons for different reasons, including illness, transport difficulties and lack of support at home.

What changed: The clearer version names the setting, gives examples of the “issues”, and explains the difficulty more directly.

Try this: Rewrite your two sentences so they answer these questions:

What is “this area”? What kinds of “factors” do you mean? What exactly are brands trying to decide or respond to?
