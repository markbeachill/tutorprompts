Social media advertising is important today. BTS have worked with brands and YouTubers also promote products. These campaigns get likes and comments online. My dissertation will look at this topic because young people use social media a lot and brands want to connect with them.

Single paragraph analysis

1. What the paragraph is trying to do

This paragraph is trying to introduce social media advertising as a dissertation topic. It gives examples of celebrities, YouTubers and online engagement, then explains why the topic matters for young people and brands.

2. Chain of ideas

social media advertising is important → BTS and YouTubers promote products → campaigns get likes and comments → young people use social media → brands want to connect with them

The chain is partly clear, but it is still quite general. The examples are relevant, but the paragraph does not yet explain what they show.

3. Where the chain breaks

  1. “BTS have worked with brands and YouTubers also promote products.” The reader can see two examples, but not why they are being compared. Are you looking at celebrity endorsement, influencer marketing, fan engagement, or youth consumer behaviour? Question to answer: What do BTS and YouTubers show about social media advertising?
  2. “These campaigns get likes and comments online.” This is useful, but it stops at description. The paragraph needs one more step explaining why likes and comments matter. Question to answer: Do likes and comments show attention, influence, brand loyalty, consumer engagement, or something else?
  3. “My dissertation will look at this topic…” The dissertation focus is still broad. “This topic” could mean social media advertising generally, BTS, YouTubers, young people, or brand communication. Question to answer: What exactly will your dissertation investigate?

4. Paragraph structure check

FeatureJudgementComment
Main focusPartly clearThe broad topic is social media advertising, but the specific focus is not yet clear.
Topic sentenceToo broad“Social media advertising is important today” is true, but it does not tell the reader your angle.
Examples or evidenceUsefulBTS and YouTubers are relevant examples, but they need explanation.
AnalysisNeeds one more stepThe paragraph describes what happens but does not explain what it shows.
Links between ideasUnevenThe ideas connect generally, but the links between examples, engagement and dissertation focus need strengthening.
Final focusSuddenThe dissertation aim appears at the end, but the paragraph has not built clearly towards it.

5. What works

You have chosen a relevant topic, and the examples are accessible. The paragraph also starts to connect advertising, social media use, young people and brands, which could become a useful dissertation focus.

6. What needs improving

  1. Make the paragraph’s focus more specific. At the moment, it is about social media advertising in general, but a dissertation usually needs a narrower angle.
  2. Explain what the examples show. BTS and YouTubers are not just examples of promotion; they may show how brands use famous or trusted online figures to reach young audiences.
  3. Add the missing analytical step after “likes and comments”. Explain why online engagement matters. For example, does it help brands measure attention, create interaction, or encourage young people to see advertising as part of entertainment?
