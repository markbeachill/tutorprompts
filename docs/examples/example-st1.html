User

Social media is important for advertising because many people use it.

BTS fans are active online. They share campaigns and hashtags. This creates a conflict for brands. McDonald's used BTS to reach consumers across social media platforms. The campaign showed how fan activity can increase visibility.

YouTubers also advertise products. Some adverts feel more personal because viewers know the YouTuber.

Chatbot

Paragraph structure review

Paragraph What the paragraph is trying to do Central claim clarity Structure and development Priority revision task
1 Introduce social media advertising as the broad topic. Unclear / too broad It gives a general statement, but not yet a specific argument or direction. Make the paragraph say what aspect of social media advertising the draft will focus on.
2 Use BTS/McDonald’s as an example of fan activity increasing campaign visibility. Partly clear The example is useful, but the “conflict for brands” is not explained. Clarify what the conflict is: control, authenticity, fan power, brand risk, or something else.
3 Introduce YouTubers as another example of more personal advertising. Partly clear but sudden This paragraph introduces a new type of social media advertising, but the link to the BTS paragraph is not yet clear. Explain why YouTubers belong in the same discussion as fan campaigns and brands.

Recurring pattern: The draft has useful examples, but the central claims are not yet specific enough. Each paragraph names a topic, but the reader needs clearer “so what?” points: what does each example show about advertising, audiences, brands, or social media influence?

Paragraph 1

What the paragraph is trying to do: It seems to introduce the general topic: social media matters for advertising because it has a large audience.

What works: It gives the reader a starting point. The connection between social media and advertising is easy to understand.

What needs improving: The claim is too general. “Many people use it” explains why social media is useful, but it does not yet set up the more interesting focus of the draft: fan activity, brand visibility, and personal influence.

How to improve it: Before developing this paragraph, decide what the draft is really arguing. Is it about audience reach, fan labour, brand control, influencer trust, or how advertising feels more personal online?

Student action: Write one rough sentence beginning: “In this draft, I am focusing on how social media advertising…”

Paragraph 2

What the paragraph is trying to do: It uses BTS fans and the McDonald’s campaign to show how online fan activity can help a brand gain visibility.

What works: This is the strongest paragraph structurally because it has a clear example. The movement from BTS fans → online sharing → McDonald’s campaign → increased visibility is followable.

What needs improving: The sentence “This creates a conflict for brands” is the key structural problem. The paragraph says there is a conflict, but it does not explain what the conflict is. Because of that, the reader cannot tell what the later McDonald’s example is meant to prove.

How to improve it: Do not add more evidence yet. First, make the conflict specific. For example, ask yourself: are brands benefiting from fan activity while also losing control of the message? Are fans doing promotional work without being paid? Does the campaign blur the line between fandom and advertising?

Student action: Complete this chain in your own words: Fan activity increases visibility → this helps brands because… → but it creates a conflict because…

Paragraph 3

What the paragraph is trying to do: It introduces YouTubers as another form of social media advertising, where adverts feel personal because viewers know the creator.

What works: The idea is relevant. It points towards parasocial relationships, trust, or authenticity, even though those terms do not need to be used unless they fit your course.

What needs improving: The paragraph arrives suddenly after the BTS/McDonald’s example. The reader needs to know whether YouTubers are a second example of the same argument, a contrast, or a new section.

How to improve it: Give this paragraph a clearer role. It could compare YouTubers with fan campaigns, or it could develop a new point about personal trust in advertising. At the moment, it is more like a note than a developed paragraph.

Student action: Decide the paragraph’s job by choosing one of these directions:

  1. YouTubers are similar to BTS fans because both make advertising feel social.
  2. YouTubers are different because the advert is tied to one trusted personality.
  3. YouTubers show another risk for brands because personal trust can blur advertising and friendship.

Which paragraph would you like to revise first?
