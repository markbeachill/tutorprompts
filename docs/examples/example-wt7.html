  2. I have a paragraph and want to add a source or quote.
  3. I have a quote and need help integrating it.

  5. I am not sure what counts as quote, paraphrase or plagiarism risk.

Paste the relevant paragraph, quote, source extract or attempt. If closeness or accuracy needs checking, include the original source extract too.

User

I have this source extract and my attempt to use it. Please check whether this is safe paraphrase or quotation use. Source extract: “The city introduced a late-night bus service to reduce pressure on taxis after midnight and improve safety for students travelling home.” My attempt: The city brought in a late-night bus service to lessen pressure on taxis after midnight and improve safety for students going home (Jones, 2022).

Chatbot

Source extract:

“The city introduced a late-night bus service to reduce pressure on taxis after midnight and improve safety for students travelling home.”

Your attempt:

The city brought in a late-night bus service to lessen pressure on taxis after midnight and improve safety for students going home (Jones, 2022).

Closeness, quotation status and accuracy check

This is not a safe paraphrase yet. It has a citation, which is good, but the wording and sentence structure are still too close to the source. The sentence mainly swaps a few words: “introduced” becomes “brought in”, “reduce” becomes “lessen”, and “travelling home” becomes “going home”. The order and shape of the source sentence stay almost the same.

Source wording Student wording Why this is a problem
“introduced a late-night bus service” “brought in a late-night bus service” Very close wording and same structure.
“to reduce pressure on taxis after midnight” “to lessen pressure on taxis after midnight” Mainly a synonym swap.
“improve safety for students travelling home” “improve safety for students going home” Mostly copied structure with small wording changes.

Plagiarism risk

This is a patchwriting risk: the citation shows where the idea came from, but the source’s wording and structure are still doing too much of the work. A paraphrase needs to express the idea in your own sentence structure, not just change individual words.

Quote or paraphrase decision

You have two safe options:

If the exact wording matters, use it as a direct quotation with quotation marks and a citation.

If the idea matters more than the exact wording, write a fuller paraphrase in your own structure. Put the source away first, explain the point from memory, then check back to make sure you have not changed the meaning.

Your next task

Put the source away and write the point from memory in one sentence. Try to answer: What did the city do, why did it do it, and who was meant to benefit?

Paste your revised attempt here and I will check whether it is far enough from the source while still accurate.
