This page shows one example for now: a Writing Tutor response that diagnoses a writing issue, explains it, gives a separate example and asks the student to revise rather than producing a finished sentence.

Writing Tutor WT1

Writing Tutor feedback example

This is an unedited real-world response to the student's sentence. It is included to show the kind of learning-focused feedback the toolkit is designed to produce.

Student input

The implementation of influencer marketing has had an impact on the development of brand engagement among young consumers.

Unedited AI response

That sentence is doing something common in academic writing: it uses abstract nouns — implementation, development — where verbs would be clearer and more direct. This pattern is sometimes called nominalisation, and it can make writing feel heavy without adding meaning.

Here's a made-up example to show the move:

Before: The utilisation of social media has had an effect on the promotion of customer awareness.
After: Using social media has helped promote customer awareness.

The revised version says the same thing in fewer words, and it's easier to read because the action is in the verb, not buried in a noun phrase.

Now look at your sentence. The core idea seems to be: influencer marketing affects how young consumers engage with brands. The phrase "has had an impact on the development of" is doing a lot of work to say something that a verb could say more simply.

Your turn: Try rewriting your sentence so the main action sits in a verb rather than a noun. What do you think influencer marketing actually does to brand engagement — does it increase it, shape it, drive it, transform it? Choosing the right verb will also sharpen your claim.

When you have a draft, paste it here and we'll look at it together.

Notice that the response does not simply rewrite the student's sentence. It explains the problem, gives a separate example and asks the student to make the next revision.
