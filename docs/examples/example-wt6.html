In-text and reference-list cross-check

You have not specified your course referencing guide or said use the toolkit house style, so I am not treating this as a final Harvard-formatted reference list. I can still cross-check citation/reference matching, which WT6 asks me to do when both in-text citations and a reference list are provided.

In-text citation Reference list entry Mismatch
Kozinets, 2002 Kozinets, R. (2012) 'The field behind the screen', Journal of Marketing Research. Date mismatch. Your text cites 2002, but the reference list says 2012. If this is the well-known netnography article, it was first published in 2002 in Journal of Marketing Research, volume 39, issue 1, with the longer title The Field behind the Screen: Using Netnography for Marketing Research in Online Communities. (journals.sagepub.com)
Hackley, 2003 No matching entry Missing from reference list. Add the Hackley source you actually used. Be careful: there may be more than one possible Hackley source around this period, so check the exact title, year and publisher rather than guessing.
No in-text citation Jones, S., Patel, R. and Lim, K. (2015) Digital Discourse. London: Routledge. Listed but not cited. Either cite Jones, Patel and Lim in the text, or remove this from the reference list if you did not use it.

Details needing checking

The Kozinets entry needs checking and probably correcting. It is missing fuller article details such as full title, volume, issue, page range and DOI. The publisher page confirms the 2002 article details and DOI, so use that to rebuild the reference according to your required Harvard guide. (journals.sagepub.com)

The Hackley citation needs a full matching reference. I would not assume the source from “Hackley, 2003” alone. For example, Taylor & Francis lists Marketing and Social Construction: Exploring the Rhetorics of Managed Consumption by Chris Hackley, with first publication in 2001 and ebook publication in 2003; Royal Holloway lists that book as 2001. That means you need to check which Hackley text your citation actually refers to. (taylorfrancis.com)
