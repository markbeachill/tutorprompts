Tool directory

Choose the right tool for the job.

This page lists every tool in the toolkit. Use it when you are not sure which mini library to download or which prompt to run once the library is uploaded. The tools are mainly for writing, revision, academic thinking, research planning and study workflow, not general homework answers.

Writing Tutor Library

Use this library when you want help with sentences, paragraphs, mistakes, style, teaching materials, referencing, paraphrase or quotation use.

CodeToolUse this when…Best input
WT1Clarity ClinicYou want help improving one sentence, a few sentences or one paragraph without handing over the writing.One sentence, a few sentences or one paragraph.
WT2Single Paragraph AnalysisYou want to know whether one paragraph has a clear point, topic sentence, development and flow.One paragraph.
WT3Find My MistakesYou want mistakes identified and explained, including grammar, logic, clarity, factual issues and referencing problems.A paragraph, section or short draft.
WT4Teach Me This MistakeYou have already run Find My Mistakes and want a teaching sheet on one repeated mistake type.The previous mistake analysis plus a chosen mistake number or type.
WT5Style and Clarity ReviewYou want clearer, livelier writing between an academic and journalistic register, avoiding dead “AI academese”.A paragraph, section or short draft.
WT6Referencing HelperYou want help creating or checking references, while remembering that Harvard and other styles vary by course.Your institution guide if available, plus full source details or links.
WT7Paraphrase and Quotation WorkshopYou want to check whether a paraphrase or quotation is accurate, safely credited and integrated into your writing.The source extract, your attempt, surrounding sentences if available, and the citation you plan to use.

Academic Thinking Tutor Library

Use this library when the issue is argument, evidence, concepts, analysis, objections or discussion.

CodeToolUse this when…Best input
AT1Assignment Brief CheckerYou want to check whether the work is actually answering the assignment question.The assignment brief plus your plan or draft.
AT2Argument MapYou want the main claim, supporting points, evidence, assumptions and gaps mapped out.A paragraph, section or essay plan.
AT3Descriptive vs Analytical CheckYou want to know whether the writing mainly describes or actually analyses.A paragraph, section or short draft.
AT4Evidence Gap CheckerYou want to find claims that need sources, examples or stronger support.A paragraph, section or short draft.
AT5Concept Clarity CheckerYou want to check whether key terms are defined and used consistently.A section using important concepts or keywords.
AT6Literature Use CheckerYou want to know whether sources are being used, compared and connected to the argument, rather than just listed.A literature review section or source-heavy paragraph.
AT7Counterargument and Limitations CheckerYou want to identify objections, limitations and weaknesses before a marker or reader does.An argument, essay section or proposal section.
AT8Source Reliability CheckerYou want a preliminary check of whether sources look suitable, credible and relevant.A source list with as much detail as possible.
AT9Critical Opponent ReviewYou want a sceptical, opposing, picky, methodological, real-world or ideological critic to challenge your argument.Your argument, paragraph, claim or proposal section.
AT10Socratic TutorYou want to discuss a topic through one-question-at-a-time tutoring, or ask the tutor to choose a useful starting point from your work.A topic, question, idea or pasted section.

Precision before polish

These tools should not make work sound more academic by changing the student’s concepts. If a suggested term may change the meaning, the tutor should explain the difference and ask the student to choose.

WT5 update

WT5 style-and-clarity feedback now emphasises location, reader effect, the move to make, and student revision prompts before any model wording.
