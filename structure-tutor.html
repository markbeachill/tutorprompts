Structure library

Structure Tutor Library

This library helps students see whether paragraphs and whole drafts are organised clearly. It checks central claims, development, sequence and links rather than simply polishing sentences.

Download this librarySee examplesBack to tools

What students can get from it

This library gives structured feedback and questions. It does not aim to produce a finished assignment. The student should decide what to use and revise the work themselves.

Tools included

ToolUse it when...What you get
Paragraph Structure Review Across a Whole DraftUse it with several paragraphs or a full short section.A table showing what each paragraph is doing, whether its central claim is clear, and what to fix first.
Whole-Work Structure ReviewUse it when a draft feels badly ordered or unbalanced.Structure map, main issues and suggested order.
Expert Meaning ReviewUse it when the ideas may not make sense.Issues with meaning, interpretation and argument.

Mini example

Student input

Paragraph 1 gives background on social media. Paragraph 2 jumps to BTS and McDonald’s. Paragraph 3 explains netnography. Paragraph 4 gives the aim.

Typical tutor response

The structure has useful parts, but the order may confuse the reader. The method appears before the research aim is clearly established. A clearer order would be: topic and problem, research aim/question, key cases, then methodology.

What this shows

The tool comments on sequence and reader orientation rather than rewriting the draft.

This is a short illustration. Actual outputs are longer, more structured and in Markdown.
