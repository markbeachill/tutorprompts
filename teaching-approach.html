Teaching approach

Feedback should teach, not replace the student.

The toolkit is built around diagnosis, explanation, practice and reflection. It is designed to help students improve their own work, not outsource it.

The learning cycle

StageWhat happensWhy it matters
DiagnosisThe tutor identifies a problem or pattern.The student sees what needs attention.
ExplanationThe tutor explains the issue in plain English.The student understands why it matters.
ExamplesThe tutor shows similar examples and corrections.The student sees how the pattern works.
PracticeThe student can answer short practice questions.The student applies the idea.
RevisionThe student rewrites their own work.The student keeps authorship and responsibility.

Mini teaching example

Original

This study show how advertisements affect online engagement.

Tutor explanation

The subject is singular: “This study”. The verb needs to match it, so “show” becomes “shows”.

Correction

This study shows how advertisements affect online engagement.

The teaching-materials tool can turn this kind of diagnosis into a short lesson with examples, practice questions and answers.

Precision and exactness

Clear writing is not only shorter or simpler writing. It must also say exactly what the student means. This principle draws on Orwell’s concern with vague or inflated language, Williams’ focus on clear characters and actions, and plain English principles of reader-centred accuracy.

The toolkit therefore discourages “academicising” that swaps a student’s key terms for smoother but different terms. If a change may alter the meaning, the tutor should explain the difference and ask the student to choose.

Paragraph logic before polish

For paragraph work, the toolkit asks students to connect the chain of ideas before polishing the topic sentence. The aim is to make the paragraph mean more clearly, not just sound smoother.

A paragraph may need better wording, but wording is not the first question. The first question is whether the reader can follow the claim, the evidence, the explanation and the link between them. If the central claim or chain of meaning is unclear, polish can hide the problem rather than solve it.
