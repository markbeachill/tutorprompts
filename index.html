Purpose

Use AI as a tutor, not a ghost-writer.

These Markdown prompt libraries help students get feedback, explanation, challenge and revision support while keeping responsibility for their own writing and thinking.

How it works

Download one toolkit file. Choose the kind of help you need.

Download the mini library that matches the kind of help you need. Upload it to an AI tool, type prompt, choose a menu item, then paste the work you want help with.

Important

A quick privacy and responsibility note

This toolkit is designed to help you learn from your own writing. For ordinary extracts of your own work, use the feedback to revise the work yourself and follow your course rules on AI use.

Be more careful with anything private or about other people. Do not paste or upload names, student numbers, email addresses, interview transcripts, placement notes, client details, case studies, unpublished research, or confidential material unless you have checked your course, research ethics, or institution rules.

For lecturers, tutors, supervisors, and others supporting students: be especially careful before pasting student work, marks, feedback, or personal information into a public AI tool. Check your assessment, data protection, and institution rules first.

What this toolkit can and cannot do

This toolkit gives students structured prompts for feedback, explanation, practice and revision planning. It is designed for students who want to learn from their own work.

It cannot prevent misuse. A student who wants AI to do the work can bypass these prompts. The value of the toolkit is that it makes responsible, learning-focused AI support easier.

The Markdown libraries are organised so the AI can focus on one tool at a time. AI systems do not execute these files like software, so behaviour may vary, especially on free plans. If the AI uses the wrong tool or gives too much information, type prompt to return to the menu, or ask it to use only the named tool code.

Paragraph logic before polish

For paragraph work, the toolkit now asks students to connect the chain of ideas before polishing the topic sentence. The aim is to make the paragraph mean more clearly, not just sound smoother.

Precision before polish

The toolkit treats precision as part of clarity. A clearer sentence is only better if it preserves or sharpens the student’s intended meaning.

Students should not choose words simply because they sound more academic. The tools should flag important choices, such as groups versus communities, or celebrities versus influencers, when the wording may change the idea.

Using the toolkit on a free AI plan

Free AI plans work best with small, clean extracts. A sentence, a paragraph, one section, or around 800 words is usually easier than a whole document.

Plain text or Markdown can make text easier for an AI tool to read. It does not make the content private. Follow the privacy and responsibility note above.

  • Best simple option: copy the relevant section and paste it as plain text.
  • Google Docs: if your work is already in Google Docs, you can use Google’s Markdown features.
  • LibreOffice Writer: can open and save Markdown files locally, which avoids unknown online converters.
