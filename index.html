Purpose

Use AI as a tutor, not a ghost-writer.

These toolkit files help students get feedback, explanation, challenge and revision support while keeping responsibility for their own writing and thinking.

This toolkit helps you use ordinary AI tools as a tutor, not as a ghost-writer. It gives the AI clearer rules so it focuses on feedback, explanation, questions and revision support rather than simply producing finished answers for you.

Important

A quick privacy and responsibility note

This toolkit is designed to help you learn from your own writing. For ordinary extracts of your own work, use the feedback to revise the work yourself and follow your course rules on AI use.

Be more careful with anything private or about other people. Do not paste or upload names, student numbers, email addresses, interview transcripts, placement notes, client details, case studies, unpublished research, or confidential material unless you have checked your course, research ethics, or institution rules.

For lecturers, tutors, supervisors, and others supporting students: be especially careful before pasting student work, marks, feedback, or personal information into a public AI tool. Check your assessment, data protection, and institution rules first.

What this toolkit can and cannot do

This toolkit gives students structured prompts for feedback, explanation, practice and revision planning. It is designed for students who want to learn from their own work.

It cannot prevent misuse. A student who wants AI to do the work can bypass these prompts. The value of the toolkit is that it makes responsible, learning-focused AI support easier.

The toolkit files are organised so the AI can focus on one tool at a time. AI systems do not execute these files like software, so behaviour may vary, especially on free plans. If the AI uses the wrong tool or gives too much information, type prompt to return to the menu, or ask it to use only the named tool code.

Using the toolkit on a free AI plan

Free AI plans work best with small, clean extracts and the smallest toolkit file that fits the task.
